## Locations to Create (8 new locations)

### 1. Split, Croatia

**Country:** Croatia
**Description:** Historic Dalmatian coast city featuring ancient Roman ruins, with the 1700-year-old Diocletian's Palace at its heart. Marble-lined streets and waterfront cafes create a vibrant Mediterranean atmosphere.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Diocletian's Palace - 1700-year-old Roman palace, one of the most important architectural heritage sites of the Roman Empire
2. Cathedral of St. Domnius - One of the best-preserved ancient Roman buildings with a bell tower offering sweeping vistas
3. Marjan Hill Forest Park - Green oasis with hiking, biking, and 360° views of Split's cityscape and Adriatic Sea

**LGBT Venues:**

1. X Club Split - First official gay bar in Split (opened June 2023), safe and inclusive space with live DJs
2. Academia Club Ghetto (Ghetto Bar) - Most popular bar for the gay community, known for bohemian vibe
3. Kocka (Dice) - LGBTQ-friendly underground club hosting drag shows, concerts, and artistic programs

---

### 2. Valletta, Malta

**Country:** Malta
**Description:** Mediterranean fortress city featuring baroque architecture and rich Knights of St. John heritage. This pocket-sized capital offers stunning harbor views and centuries of history within fortress walls.

**Top Attractions:**

1. St. John's Co-Cathedral - Built 1573-1577, features two Caravaggio masterpieces and stunning baroque interior
2. Upper Barrakka Gardens - Beautiful gardens with amazing Grand Harbour views and daily cannon firing at noon and 4pm
3. Grand Master's Palace - Richly decorated state rooms with 18th-century frescos and historic Palace Armoury

**LGBT Venues:**

1. Michelangelo Club - Only gay nightclub on Malta island, located in St. Julian's with 2 dance floors
2. The Thirsty Barber - Gay-friendly bar in Valletta's heart with superb cocktails and drag shows
3. LOLLIPOP - Malta's biggest queer event playing pop, disco, and house music (pop-up events)

---

### 3. Palermo, Sicily, Italy

**Country:** Italy
**Description:** Sicilian capital showcasing Byzantine mosaics, royal palaces, and authentic markets. This vibrant city blends Arab-Norman architecture with Mediterranean charm and rich cultural heritage.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Cappella Palatina (Palatine Chapel) - Palermo's top attraction designed by Roger II in 1130, famous for gold Byzantine mosaics
2. Cattedrale di Palermo - Most beautiful and important church in the city, showcasing Arab-Norman-Gothic architectural blend
3. Teatro Massimo - Largest opera house in Italy and second-largest in Europe, hosting recitals, ballet, and concerts

**LGBT Venues:**

1. EXIT10&LOVE (Exit Drinks) - Historic venue (25+ years) with DJ sets and drag shows at Piazza S. Francesco di Paola
2. Fabric Club House - Saturday night institution managed by Exit Drinks, offering weekly parties with animation and music
3. Cha Bar - Gay-friendly bar and tea room perfect for socializing in a comfortable environment

---

### 4. Sorrento, Italy

**Country:** Italy
**Description:** Charming Amalfi Coast town providing access to Pompeii, Capri, and coastal villages. Perched on dramatic cliffs with lemon groves and stunning Bay of Naples views.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Piazza Tasso - Historic town square and beating heart of Sorrento with quaint cafes and shops
2. Marina Grande - Waterfront fishing village with seafood restaurants and beach atmosphere
3. Villa Comunale Gardens & Church of San Francesco - 14th-century church and clifftop gardens with romantic Bay of Naples views

**LGBT Venues:**

1. No dedicated LGBT venues - Sorrento has no organized gay scene, but all venues are gay-friendly
2. Nearby Naples (20 min) - Depot Napoli fetish cruising bar for organized nightlife
3. Anatema Party - LGBTQ+ event at Club Shine in Naples every Saturday

---

### 5. Civitavecchia (Rome), Italy

**Country:** Italy
**Description:** Historic port city serving as gateway to Rome's ancient landmarks. Home to iconic attractions including the Colosseum, Vatican, Trevi Fountain, and 2,000+ years of history.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Fort Michelangelo - Imposing Renaissance fortress completed by Michelangelo in 1557, guarding against pirate invasions
2. Taurine Baths (Terme di Traiano) - Preserved ancient Roman bathing complex with mosaics and marble decorations
3. Piazza Leandra - Civitavecchia's oldest square in the medieval district with centuries-old landmarks

**LGBT Venues (Rome):**

1. Coming Out Bar - LGBTQ+ hub since 2001 next to the Colosseum, open all day with themed nights and drag shows
2. Muccassassina - Most popular and iconic LGBTQ+ event in Italy with outdoor setting during warm season
3. Company Roma - Lively bear bar with welcoming atmosphere, creative cocktails, and karaoke nights

---

### 6. Villefranche (Nice), France

**Country:** France
**Description:** Colorful French Riviera town offering proximity to Monaco and Nice. Charming waterfront village with historic passageways and Mediterranean beauty.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Rue Obscure (Dark Street) - 130m underground passageway dating back to 1260, originally a military thoroughfare
2. Saint Michel's Church - 18th-century Baroque church with iconic yellow bell tower visible throughout town
3. Chapelle Saint-Pierre (Cocteau Chapel) - Small chapel with walls and ceilings covered in Jean Cocteau frescos

**LGBT Venues (Nice):**

1. The BLITZ Bar - Opened 2023, already a reference for atmosphere, open Wed-Sun 6pm-2am
2. Le Glam - Most popular gay club in Nice, hopping on weekends, open Fri-Sun 11:30pm-4:30am
3. Le Six (Le 6) - Oldest gay bar (since 2006), famous for shower shows and international DJs, open Tue-Sat 10pm-5am

---

### 7. Marseille, France

**Country:** France
**Description:** Vibrant waterfront city serving as gateway to Provence. Features historic Vieux Port where ships have docked for millennia, plus stunning modern architecture.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Notre-Dame de la Garde - Opulent 19th-century basilica on city's highest point with 360° panorama views
2. Vieux Port (Old Port) - Marseille's birthplace where the city began as Greek port around 600 BCE, focal point with seafood restaurants
3. MuCEM - Stunning museum exploring Mediterranean history and culture with fascinating modern architecture

**LGBT Venues:**

1. The New Cancan - Marseille's longest-running gay dance club, well-known beyond city borders
2. L'Annexe Bar - Marseille's new gay and gay-friendly bar
3. Le Cargo - Most famous gay sauna and club venue in Marseille

---

### 8. Barcelona, Spain

**Country:** Spain
**Description:** Spain's gay capital concluding the voyage. Features Gaudí's architectural masterpieces, Mediterranean beaches, and thriving LGBTQ+ scene in the famous "Gaixample" district.

**Top Attractions:**

1. Sagrada Família - Gaudí's architectural masterpiece and Barcelona's most famous building, world's tallest church upon completion (2026)
2. Park Güell - UNESCO World Heritage Site and Gaudí fantasy masterpiece with famous mosaic steps and city views
3. Casa Batlló - One of Europe's strangest residential buildings, Gaudí at his fantastical best with marine-world themes

**LGBT Venues:**

1. El Cangrejo - Cornerstone of Barcelona's LGBTQ+ nightlife for decades, famous for electric atmosphere and drag shows
2. Arena Madre - Popular hotspot with younger crowd enjoying techno beats and striptease shows
3. Priscilla Cafe/BELIEVE Club - Most famous drag queen club in Gaixample with live music, karaoke, and drag shows
